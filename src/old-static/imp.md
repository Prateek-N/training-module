# Month 1 Training Program (21-Day Expert Development Plan)

## Objective

The objective of this 21-day training program is to transform every newly hired expert into a confident, knowledgeable, process-oriented, and independent professional who can handle candidates throughout their entire interview journey with minimal supervision.

This program is not designed to simply teach Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs). Instead, it builds a complete understanding of the company's business model, the recruitment lifecycle, candidate psychology, technical interview preparation, communication standards, documentation practices, and operational discipline.

By the end of this training, every expert should be capable of independently supporting candidates during resume preparation, technical interviews, mock interviews, online assessments, and client interactions while maintaining the quality standards defined by the organization.

---

# Why This Training Program Exists

A candidate's success depends on much more than technical knowledge. Every interaction between the candidate and our team influences interview performance and client satisfaction.

An expert who lacks knowledge in even one area can negatively impact:

* Candidate confidence
* Interview performance
* Client relationships
* Company reputation
* Internal productivity

To prevent these issues, every expert must first understand **why** each process exists before learning **how** to execute it.

The training follows the same journey that every candidate experiences inside the company, allowing the expert to understand the complete workflow rather than isolated tasks.

---

# Training Philosophy

The training is designed around four stages of learning:

### Stage 1 – Learn

The expert first develops theoretical knowledge about the US recruitment industry, employment eligibility, company workflow, and operational standards.

Without understanding the foundation, practical execution becomes mechanical and error-prone.

---

### Stage 2 – Observe

Before performing any task independently, the expert observes experienced team members handling real candidates.

Observation allows the expert to understand:

* Communication style
* Candidate handling
* Documentation
* Common mistakes
* Problem-solving approaches
* Real-world scenarios

This minimizes mistakes during practical execution.

---

### Stage 3 – Practice Under Supervision

Once the expert understands the process, they begin performing tasks under the guidance of Team Leads and experienced mentors.

Mistakes are expected during this phase.

The purpose is to provide continuous feedback until the expert develops consistency and confidence.

---

### Stage 4 – Independent Execution

Only after successfully completing supervised practice does the expert transition to independent task handling.

By this stage, they should be able to perform every responsibility according to company SOPs without requiring continuous supervision.

# Why the Training Starts with Time Zones

Every interview, assessment, client meeting, and recruiter communication follows US business hours.

If an expert misunderstands time zones:

* Interviews may be scheduled incorrectly.
* Candidates may miss interviews.
* Clients may lose confidence.
* Internal teams may face unnecessary escalations.

Therefore, understanding EST, CST, MST, PST, and Daylight Saving Time is the first building block of operational excellence. 

---

# Why Visa Knowledge Is Important

Every candidate has a different work authorization.

The marketing strategy, job submissions, sponsorship requirements, and client eligibility all depend on the candidate's visa status.

An expert who cannot differentiate between:

* OPT
* STEM OPT
* H1B
* GC
* GC-EAD
* CPT
* L1
* H4

cannot properly assist candidates or communicate accurately with recruiters.

Understanding visa categories allows the expert to:

* Explain work authorization.
* Understand employment restrictions.
* Identify sponsorship requirements.
* Provide accurate guidance during interviews. 

---

# Why We Teach the Company Workflow

Many employees only understand their own department.

This often creates communication gaps between teams.

Instead, every expert should understand the complete candidate lifecycle.

## Sales Team

Sales identifies potential candidates and explains company services.

Their goal is to onboard qualified candidates.

---

## Resume Team

The Resume Team rebuilds the candidate's resume according to current market requirements.

Their work determines how the candidate will be presented to clients.

---

## Marketing Team

Marketing submits candidate profiles to recruiters and clients.

They schedule interviews and generate job opportunities.

Without marketing, candidates cannot secure interviews.

---

## Technical Team

The Technical Team prepares candidates for every stage after interviews begin.

Responsibilities include:

* Resume Understanding
* Project explanations
* Technical preparation
* Mock interviews
* Assessment support
* Interview support
* Candidate improvement plans

This team plays the largest role in improving candidate interview performance.

---

# Why Resume Understanding Comes First

A candidate cannot confidently answer interview questions if they do not fully understand their own resume.

Resume Understanding teaches candidates:

* Project architecture
* Technologies used
* Business requirements
* Individual responsibilities
* Technical terminology
* Project flow

Only after mastering their resume can candidates confidently attend interviews.

---

# Why Mock Interviews Are Conducted

Mock interviews simulate real interview environments.

The objective is not only to ask questions but also to evaluate:

* Confidence
* Communication
* Technical knowledge
* Thinking process
* Behavioral responses
* Weak areas

After evaluation, a personalized improvement plan is created to help candidates become interview-ready.

---

# Why Assessment Support Is Important

Many clients conduct online assessments before interviews.

These assessments often include:

* Coding
* Aptitude
* Logical reasoning
* English comprehension
* System design

Experts must understand:

* Platform restrictions
* Recording policies
* Screen sharing
* Remote support
* Access protocols
* Escalation procedures

Proper assessment support significantly improves candidate success rates while ensuring compliance with company SOPs. 

---

# Why Client Interaction Training Matters

Technical knowledge alone is insufficient.

Experts also represent the company during client interactions.

Professional communication builds trust and ensures that information is exchanged accurately.

Training focuses on:

* Professional etiquette
* Clear communication
* Accurate documentation
* Candidate updates
* Client expectations

This reduces misunderstandings and strengthens business relationships.

---

# Why Interview Support Training Is Essential

Interview support is one of the most critical responsibilities.

Experts learn to:

* Prepare candidates before interviews.
* Follow the 30-20-10 communication protocol.
* Verify audio and screen sharing.
* Update live documentation.
* Assist candidates professionally throughout the interview process.

The objective is to create a seamless interview experience for both the candidate and the client.

---

# Why We Focus on SOP Mastery

Knowing the process is different from consistently following it.

SOP Mastery ensures that experts can perform every task exactly as defined by company standards without missing critical steps.

Independent execution demonstrates readiness for real assignments.

---

# Why Zero-Error Performance Is Required

Small mistakes often have significant consequences.

Examples include:

* Missing interview reminders
* Forgetting recordings
* Poor documentation
* Delayed updates
* Incomplete feedback
* Communication failures

Even a single error can affect the candidate's interview outcome or damage the company's reputation.

For this reason, the final stage emphasizes operational discipline and consistency.

Experts are expected to:

* Maintain complete documentation.
* Follow all SOPs.
* Meet deadlines.
* Communicate proactively.
* Deliver high-quality support every time. 

---

# Expected Outcome After 21 Days

By the end of the training program, every expert should be able to:

* Understand the US recruitment ecosystem.
* Explain visa categories and work authorization confidently.
* Understand the complete company workflow from Sales to Technical Support.
* Prepare candidates through Resume Understanding sessions.
* Conduct structured mock interviews.
* Support online assessments according to SOPs.
* Handle screening and technical interview support.
* Interact professionally with candidates and internal stakeholders.
* Maintain accurate documentation and follow company SOPs.
* Work independently with minimal supervision while maintaining zero-error operational standards.

## Conclusion

This 21-day program is designed to develop complete professionals—not just task executors. Each day's training builds upon the previous one, ensuring that by the end of the month, the expert understands not only **how** to perform each responsibility but also **why** every process exists and how it contributes to the candidate's success and the company's overall quality standards.
